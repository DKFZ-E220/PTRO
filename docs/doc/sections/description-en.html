The Ontology for Preclinical Trials in Radiation Oncology (PTRO) is a domain-specific ontology developed to support the integration of SEND-standardized preclinical data. It provides semantic representations for key aspects of preclinical studies, including:

a) Demographics – animal characteristics such as species, strain, age, and sex.
b) Observational notes – study-related remarks and annotations.
c) Treatment exposure – administered interventions, including radiation dosages and therapeutic agents.
d) Body weight records – measurements collected throughout the study and at its conclusion.
e) Mortality diagnoses – documentation of causes of death.
f) Laboratory test results – data generated from laboratory analyses.
g) Clinical observations – symptoms and health assessments recorded during the study period.
h) Procedures – details of experimental interventions performed on individual animals.

The ontology was created using a semi-systematic methodology that combines manual extraction of core concepts from SEND-standardized datasets, reuse and integration of existing ontologies (e.g., ROO [REF], NCIT [REF]), and incorporates the Semantic Types Ontology (STY) to ensure broad concept categorization and semantic consistency.
