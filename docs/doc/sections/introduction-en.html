Introduction back to ToC

Preclinical radiation oncology studies generate complex datasets that are often heterogeneous and difficult to integrate, interpret, and reuse. To address this challenge, we developed the Ontology for Preclinical Trials in Radiation Oncology (PTRO), a domain-specific ontology that provides a semantic framework for representing preclinical data. PTRO captures key study components including animal demographics, treatment exposure, body weight records, mortality diagnoses, laboratory test results, clinical observations, and experimental procedures. PTRO supports integration with platforms such as RadPlanBio [1] and deployment within Virtual Knowledge Graphs, enabling flexible querying, reasoning, and cross-study data analysis. The ontology, along with its SPARQL mappings and queries, is publicly available on GitHub [2].
