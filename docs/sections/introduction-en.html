Introduction back to ToC

The Preclinical Trials in Radiation Oncology Ontology (PTRO) [1,2], offers a semantic framework specifically designed to represent SEND-standardized preclinical data in the radiation oncology domain. It provides formal representations of essential study components, including animal demographics, treatment exposures, body weight records, mortality assessments, laboratory results, clinical observations, and experimental procedures.

PTRO has been developed using a semi-systematic methodology that integrates three key steps: (i) identification and extraction of core concepts from SEND datasets [3], (ii) reuse and alignment with established ontologies such as the Radiation Oncology Ontology (ROO) [4] and the NCI Thesaurus (NCIT) [5], and (iii) iterative refinement based on domain expert feedback. Additionally, the Semantic Types Ontology (STY) [6], is incorporated to ensure high-level categorization and semantic coherence.

By promoting interoperability, semantic consistency, and data reuse, PTRO supports the implementation of FAIR principles [7]. It further enables flexible querying and integration of preclinical data within virtual knowledge graph environments.
