Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

PTRO focuses on preclinical data standardized according to SEND, with emphasis on radiation oncology studies. It is not intended to replace clinical trial ontologies or general-purpose biomedical ontologies but to complement them by addressing the specific needs of preclinical research data representation.


PTRO is designed to:

  • Support FAIR data practices (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable).
  • Facilitate flexible querying within virtual knowledge graph environments.
  • Enable researchers to integrate, compare, and reuse preclinical trial data across studies and institutions.
  • Provide a semantic layer for linking SEND data with related biomedical ontologies.


Introduction back to ToC

The Preclinical Trials in Radiation Oncology Ontology (PTRO) [1,2], offers a semantic framework specifically designed to represent SEND-standardized preclinical data in the radiation oncology domain. It provides formal representations of essential study components, including animal demographics, treatment exposures, body weight records, mortality assessments, laboratory results, clinical observations, and experimental procedures.

PTRO has been developed using a semi-systematic methodology that integrates three key steps: (i) identification and extraction of core concepts from SEND datasets [3], (ii) reuse and alignment with established ontologies such as the Radiation Oncology Ontology (ROO) [4] and the NCI Thesaurus (NCIT) [5], and (iii) iterative refinement based on domain expert feedback. Additionally, the Semantic Types Ontology (STY) [6], is incorporated to ensure high-level categorization and semantic coherence.

By promoting interoperability, semantic consistency, and data reuse, PTRO supports the implementation of FAIR principles [7]. It further enables flexible querying and integration of preclinical data within virtual knowledge graph environments.

: Description back to ToC

PTRO models key aspects of preclinical studies, including:

  • Animal demographics
  • Treatment exposures
  • Body weight records
  • Mortality diagnoses
  • Laboratory test results
  • Clinical observations
  • Experimental procedures

The ontology was developed through a semi-systematic methodology combining:

  1. Extraction of core concepts from SEND datasets.
  2. Reuse and alignment with existing ontologies, such as the Radiation Oncology Ontology (ROO) and the NCI Thesaurus (NCIT).
  3. Iterative refinement with input from domain experts.

The Semantic Types Ontology (STY) is integrated to provide high-level categorization and ensure semantic consistency across the ontology.
